About

Reading the data myself

I was told I had no vision for my future, so I decided to focus on visualisation instead.

I'm an economics student, and I question the validity of the field almost every day. Rather than keep arguing about whether it holds up, I've decided to find out for myself, by learning to work with the data directly instead of talking around it.

So I've been teaching myself to decode large public datasets, and to use the economics and econometric methods I find genuinely interesting and practical, not just the ones that look elegant on a page.

More than opinion pieces, I want to listen to what the data is actually saying and build visual stories that sit right with the numbers. I write what the data tells me, not what I wish it said. And I try to be honest about the rest: the constraints of handling messy, massive datasets, the struggles along the way, and why I made the decisions I did. The choices are part of the story, not something to hide.

This site is where that work lives. If any of it interests you, I'd like to hear from you.
